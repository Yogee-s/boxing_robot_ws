# 8-Week Intermediate Boxing Training Plan

This plan is for boxers who have completed the beginner program or have equivalent experience: solid fundamentals in stance, jab, cross, hook, basic defense, and foundational footwork. The intermediate plan adds uppercuts, body work, longer combinations, advanced defense, and increased conditioning intensity.

## Prerequisites
- Clean 1-2-3-2 combination on the bag
- Basic blocking and parrying ability
- Ability to complete 3x3-minute rounds with 1-minute rest
- Understanding of step-drag footwork

## Equipment Needed
- Boxing gloves (14-16 oz for bag, 16 oz for sparring)
- Hand wraps
- Jump rope
- Heavy bag, double-end bag (or BoxBunny robot)
- Timer (3-minute rounds, 1-minute rest)

## Week 1-2: Uppercuts and Level Changes

### Goals
- Develop clean uppercut technique (lead and rear)
- Integrate body shots into existing combinations
- Increase round duration endurance

### Session Structure (4x per week)
1. **Warm-up (10 min)**: Jump rope 3x2 min (include crossovers and double-unders), 2 rounds shadow boxing
2. **Uppercut Technique (15 min)**: Mirror work for lead and rear uppercuts. Heavy bag close work: 3 rounds of uppercuts only.
3. **Level Change Combos (15 min)**: 1-2-5 (jab-cross-lead uppercut), 1b-2 (body jab-head cross), 3b-3 (body hook-head hook). 2 rounds each on the bag.
4. **Defense Review (10 min)**: Blocking and parrying drills from beginner plan at increased speed.
5. **Conditioning (10 min)**: 4 rounds -- 15 push-ups, 20 squats, 15 sit-ups, 10 burpees
6. **Cool-down (5 min)**

### BoxBunny Drills
- Shadow Sparring: 1-2-5 and 1b-2 sequences
- Defence Drill (intermediate): 2 rounds

## Week 3-4: Slipping and Counter-Punching

### Goals
- Develop slip technique (inside and outside)
- Learn to counter after slipping
- Chain defense into offense seamlessly

### Session Structure (4x per week)
1. **Warm-up (10 min)**: Jump rope 3x2 min, 2 rounds shadow boxing with head movement
2. **Slip Drill (15 min)**: Rope slip drill (3x2 min). Partner jab drill: slip outside, counter with cross. Slip inside, counter with lead hook. 3 rounds each.
3. **Combination with Defense (15 min)**: 1-2-Slip-2 (jab-cross-slip-cross). 1-2-3-Slip-2 (jab-cross-hook-slip-cross). 3 rounds on the bag integrating slips between punches.
4. **Double-End Bag (10 min)**: 3 rounds. Jab the bag, slip the rebound, counter with the cross. Develops timing and reactive defense.
5. **Conditioning (10 min)**: 5 rounds circuit with increased intensity
6. **Cool-down (5 min)**

### BoxBunny Drills
- Defence Drill (intermediate): 3 rounds -- focus on slipping robot arm punches
- Reaction Time Drill: Measure improvement in slip timing

## Week 5-6: Combination Depth and Pressure

### Goals
- Execute 5+ punch combinations with proper form
- Develop ring pressure (controlling space)
- Build sustained output capacity (punch volume over rounds)

### Session Structure (4-5x per week)
1. **Warm-up (10 min)**: Jump rope 4x2 min (alternate fast/slow intervals), 2 rounds shadow boxing
2. **Long Combination Work (20 min)**: Intermediate combos 1-10 on the bag. 4 rounds: each round, work through 2-3 combinations. Focus on maintaining form through the full sequence.
3. **Pressure Drill (10 min)**: Shadow box with constant forward movement. Jab every 2 steps. Never stop moving forward. This builds the habit of applying pressure.
4. **Cutting the Ring (10 min)**: Partner moves laterally. Practice cutting off their movement with diagonal steps and jabs.
5. **Conditioning (10 min)**: 5 rounds circuit -- add medicine ball work and plank variations
6. **Cool-down (5 min)**

### BoxBunny Drills
- Shadow Sparring: Full intermediate combination set with timing constraints
- Defence Drill (intermediate): 3 rounds with mixed attack patterns

## Week 7-8: Integration and Flow

### Goals
- Flow between offense, defense, and footwork without pauses
- Develop combination improvisation (adapting mid-sequence)
- Build toward sparring readiness

### Session Structure (4-5x per week)
1. **Warm-up (10 min)**: Jump rope 4x2 min, 3 rounds shadow boxing (full repertoire)
2. **Free Combination Rounds (15 min)**: 4 rounds on the bag with no predetermined combos. Throw what feels right, focusing on flow and natural transitions. Mix head and body, straight and curved punches.
3. **Offense-Defense-Offense Drill (15 min)**: Throw a 1-2, slip a simulated counter, throw a 3-2. Repeat with variations. 3 rounds with a partner or on the double-end bag.
4. **Sparring Simulation (10 min)**: Light technical sparring with a partner (if available) or shadow boxing with an imaginary opponent. Focus on distance management, using the jab, and returning to guard.
5. **Conditioning (10 min)**: 6 rounds high-intensity circuit
6. **Cool-down (5 min)**

### BoxBunny Drills
- Shadow Sparring: Mixed beginner and intermediate combos, randomly ordered
- Defence Drill (intermediate): 4 rounds with increased robot speed
- Free training mode: Unrestricted rounds for flow development

## Weekly Milestone Targets

| Week | Target |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Throw 10 clean uppercuts per hand on the bag |
| 2 | Execute 1b-2 and 3b-3 level changes with visible knee bend |
| 3 | Slip 7/10 jabs thrown at 60% speed |
| 4 | Counter with a cross within 0.5 sec of slipping a jab |
| 5 | Complete a 5-punch combination on the bag without balance loss |
| 6 | Throw 200+ punches in a 3-minute round with maintained form |
| 7 | Flow between 5 different combos in a single round |
| 8 | Complete 4 rounds of offense-defense integration without gassing |

## Progression Criteria to Advanced

Before moving to advanced training:
- Execute all intermediate combinations from memory
- Slip and counter consistently against live jabs and crosses
- Maintain sustained output (150+ punches per round) for 4 rounds
- Demonstrate body-head level changes in combinations
- Show defensive movement between offensive bursts
- Comfortable with 4-round sessions at 3 minutes work / 1 minute rest
