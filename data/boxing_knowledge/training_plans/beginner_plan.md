# 8-Week Beginner Boxing Training Plan

This plan is designed for someone with little to no boxing experience. It assumes 3-4 training sessions per week, each lasting 45-60 minutes. The focus is on building proper technique, basic fitness, and the mental habits needed for progression.

## Equipment Needed
- Boxing gloves (12-16 oz)
- Hand wraps
- Jump rope
- Heavy bag (or BoxBunny robot)
- Timer (3-minute rounds, 1-minute rest)

## Week 1-2: Foundation

### Goals
- Learn orthodox/southpaw stance and guard
- Develop the jab with correct form
- Build basic conditioning

### Session Structure (3x per week)
1. **Warm-up (10 min)**: Jump rope 3x2 minutes, dynamic stretches
2. **Stance and Guard Drill (10 min)**: Mirror work, partner push test, tennis ball chin drill
3. **Jab Practice (15 min)**: 3 rounds of jab-only shadow boxing. Focus on straight line, snap-back, and rear hand staying up.
4. **Conditioning (10 min)**: 3 rounds of bodyweight circuit -- 10 push-ups, 10 squats, 10 sit-ups per round
5. **Cool-down (5 min)**: Static stretching, shoulder rolls, deep breathing

### BoxBunny Drills
- Reaction Time Drill (beginner difficulty): 2 rounds
- Shadow Sparring: Jab-only sequences

## Week 3-4: Adding the Cross

### Goals
- Develop the cross with hip rotation
- Master the 1-2 combination
- Improve footwork fundamentals

### Session Structure (3-4x per week)
1. **Warm-up (10 min)**: Jump rope 3x2 minutes with footwork variation
2. **Cross Technique (10 min)**: Heavy bag work focusing on hip rotation and weight transfer
3. **1-2 Combination (15 min)**: 3 rounds of jab-cross on the bag. Round 1 slow, Round 2 medium, Round 3 full speed.
4. **Footwork (10 min)**: Step-drag drill, box step drill, forward/backward movement
5. **Conditioning (10 min)**: 3 rounds circuit -- 15 push-ups, 15 squats, 20 sit-ups
6. **Cool-down (5 min)**

### BoxBunny Drills
- Reaction Time Drill (beginner difficulty): 2 rounds
- Shadow Sparring: 1-2 combination sequences

## Week 5-6: Introducing Hooks and Defense

### Goals
- Learn lead hook technique
- Introduce basic blocking (high guard)
- Start combining offense and defense

### Session Structure (3-4x per week)
1. **Warm-up (10 min)**: Jump rope 3x2 minutes, shadow boxing 1 round (jabs and crosses only)
2. **Hook Technique (10 min)**: Mirror work for hook rotation, then heavy bag rounds
3. **Combination Work (15 min)**: 1-2-3 combination on the bag. Alternate with 1-1-2 and 1-2-3-2.
4. **Defense Drill (10 min)**: Partner throws jabs at 50% speed, practise high guard block and parry
5. **Conditioning (10 min)**: 4 rounds circuit -- add planks (30 sec) and burpees (5 per round)
6. **Cool-down (5 min)**

### BoxBunny Drills
- Defence Drill (beginner difficulty): 2 rounds
- Shadow Sparring: 1-2-3 and 1-2-3-2 sequences

## Week 7-8: Putting It Together

### Goals
- Combine all learned punches into fluid combinations
- Develop the habit of returning to guard after every combination
- Begin light sparring awareness (distance management)

### Session Structure (4x per week)
1. **Warm-up (10 min)**: Jump rope 3x2 minutes, 2 rounds of shadow boxing with all punches
2. **Combination Rounds (15 min)**: 4 rounds on the bag. Each round focuses on a different beginner combo (1-2, 1-1-2, 1-2-3, 1-2-3-2). Mix them freely in the final round.
3. **Defense and Counter (10 min)**: Partner throws jab, you block and counter with a cross. Partner throws cross, you parry and counter with a hook.
4. **Footwork Integration (10 min)**: Shadow box with emphasis on moving after every combination. Forward, throw combo, step back. Lateral step, throw combo, pivot.
5. **Conditioning (10 min)**: 5 rounds -- push-ups, squats, sit-ups, planks, shadow boxing (30 sec each)
6. **Cool-down (5 min)**

### BoxBunny Drills
- Defence Drill (beginner difficulty): 3 rounds
- Shadow Sparring: Mixed beginner combinations
- Reaction Time Drill: Record baseline improvement from Week 1

## Weekly Milestone Targets

| Week | Target |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Hold proper stance for 3 minutes without correction |
| 2 | Throw 100 jabs in 3 minutes with good form |
| 3 | Throw a clean 1-2 with visible hip rotation |
| 4 | Move forward and backward while maintaining stance |
| 5 | Throw 1-2-3 combination with proper hook mechanics |
| 6 | Block 8/10 jabs thrown at 50% speed |
| 7 | Complete 4 rounds of combination work without gassing |
| 8 | Flow between 3 different combinations without stopping |

## Progression Criteria to Intermediate

Before moving to the intermediate plan, the user should be able to:
- Throw all beginner combinations with correct form
- Block basic straight punches consistently
- Maintain proper stance and guard throughout a 3-minute round
- Complete 3 rounds of bag work with 1-minute rest between rounds
- Demonstrate basic footwork (forward, backward, lateral)
