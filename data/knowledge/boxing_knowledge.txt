## BOXING STANCE (Orthodox)
- Lead foot (left) forward, rear foot (right) back at 45 degrees
- Feet shoulder-width apart, weight evenly distributed 50/50
- Knees slightly bent for mobility and balance
- Chin tucked, eyes forward through eyebrows
- Lead hand at chin height, rear hand protecting chin
- Elbows close to body protecting ribs

## BASIC PUNCHES

### Jab (Code 1) - Left straight
- Extend lead hand straight from chin, rotate fist palm-down on impact
- Snap back immediately to guard position
- Push off rear foot slightly, keep rear hand protecting chin
- Uses: range finder, set up combinations, control distance
- Common errors: dropping rear hand, telegraphing, over-reaching

### Cross (Code 2) - Right straight
- Drive from rear foot, rotate hips and shoulders
- Extend rear hand straight to target, palm rotates down
- Transfer weight from rear to lead foot during punch
- Most powerful straight punch due to full body rotation
- Uses: power shot, counter-punch, finish combinations

### Lead Hook (Code 3) - Left hook
- Rotate lead hip and shoulder, arm bent at 90 degrees
- Fist travels in horizontal arc to target
- Pivot on lead foot, weight shifts through rotation
- Short-range power punch targeting chin, temple, or body
- Common errors: looping too wide, dropping guard

### Rear Hook (Code 4) - Right hook
- Similar mechanics to lead hook but from rear side
- Rotate rear hip through, pivot on rear foot
- Less common but effective counter or body shot
- Uses: inside fighting, close range exchanges

### Lead Uppercut (Code 5) - Left uppercut
- Drop slightly by bending knees, keep elbow close
- Drive upward from legs through hips
- Fist rises vertically from low to high
- Effective at close range, targets chin or body
- Set up by getting inside opponent's guard

### Rear Uppercut (Code 6) - Right uppercut
- Rear hand drops slightly, drive upward through legs and hips
- Full body rotation adds significant power
- Most powerful uppercut variation
- Excellent counter when opponent ducks forward

## DEFENSIVE TECHNIQUES

### Slip
- Move head off center line to avoid punch
- Slip left to avoid jab/cross, slip right to avoid hooks
- Bend at waist and knees, keep eyes on opponent
- Creates counter-punching opportunities

### Block
- Use gloves and arms to absorb incoming punches
- High guard: both hands up protecting head
- Body block: lower elbows to protect ribs
- Parry: redirect punch with small hand movement

### Bob and Weave
- Bend knees to duck under hooks
- Move in U-shape motion: down, under, up
- Stay balanced, ready to counter immediately

## COMBINATIONS

### Basic (Beginner)
- 1-2 (Jab-Cross): Foundation combo, teaches weight transfer
- 1-1-2 (Double Jab-Cross): Range control into power
- 1-2-3 (Jab-Cross-Hook): Classic three-punch combo
- 1-2-1-2 (Double Jab-Cross): Rhythm and timing

### Intermediate
- 1-2-3-2 (Jab-Cross-Hook-Cross): Four-punch combination
- 1-2-3-4 (Jab-Cross-L Hook-R Hook): Full rotation combo
- 1-2-5-6 (Jab-Cross-L Upper-R Upper): Mix levels
- Body shot combos: 1-2b-3b (upstairs-downstairs)

### Advanced
- 1-slip-2-3 (Jab-Slip-Cross-Hook): Offense-defense flow
- 1-2-3-4-5-6 (Full six): All punches in sequence
- Counter combinations after defensive moves
- Feint-based combinations

## TRAINING STAGES (AIBA Method)

### Initiation Stage (Beginners)
- Focus on fundamental stance and guard
- Learn basic punches individually
- Develop coordination and balance
- 2-3 sessions per week, short duration
- Emphasis on fun and engagement

### Basic Stage
- Combine punches into simple combos (1-2, 1-2-3)
- Introduce basic defensive moves
- Begin pad work and light sparring
- Build aerobic base and general fitness
- 3-4 sessions per week

### Specialization Stage
- Complex combinations and counter-fighting
- Sport-specific conditioning
- Regular sparring with structured rounds
- Tactical development and fight strategy
- 4-5 sessions per week

### High-Performance Stage
- Competition preparation
- Individual technique refinement
- Peak conditioning cycles
- Mental preparation and ring psychology

## PHYSICAL CONDITIONING

### Endurance
- Aerobic base: running, skipping rope, swimming
- Boxing-specific: heavy bag rounds, shadow boxing
- Build to 3-minute round intervals with 1-minute rest
- Progressive overload over weeks

### Strength
- Core: planks, Russian twists, medicine ball
- Upper body: push-ups, pull-ups, resistance bands
- Lower body: squats, lunges, box jumps
- Boxing-specific: heavy bag power shots

### Speed
- Speed bag, double-end bag
- Reaction drills with visual/audio cues
- Fast combinations on focus pads
- Plyometric training for explosive power

### Coordination
- Jump rope (various patterns)
- Shadow boxing with footwork
- Combination drills with movement
- Mirror drills for symmetry

## TRAINING SESSION STRUCTURE
1. Warm-up (10-15 min): Light cardio, dynamic stretching
2. Technical work (20-30 min): Technique drills, combinations
3. Conditioning (15-20 min): Pad work, bag work, or sparring
4. Cool-down (10 min): Static stretching, breathing exercises

## BOXING STYLES (Advanced - from AIBA Manual Chapter 5)

### European Style
- Emphasis on technical precision and footwork
- Long-range fighting, use of jab
- Disciplined defense, counter-punching

### Russian Style
- Strong emphasis on conditioning and endurance
- Aggressive forward pressure
- Body punching combined with head attacks

### American Style
- Versatile, adaptive approach
- Strong jab and cross fundamentals
- Good defensive awareness

### Cuban Style
- Exceptional footwork and movement
- Angles and lateral movement
- Counter-punching expertise
- Unpredictable rhythm changes
